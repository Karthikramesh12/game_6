Hints

The following are inscriptions in hieroglyphic Luwian, an ancient Anatolian language related to (and once thought to be) Hittite.

These writings were totally incomprehensible until one scholar discovered the key: many of the words were names of regions, cities, and kings.

There are six inscriptions that correspond to the names of two regions (Khamatu, Palaa), two cities (Kurkuma, Tuvarnava), and two kings (Varpalava, Tarkumuva).

The process you use to solve this puzzle is very similar to what archeological linguists actually do when they discover writings and inscriptions in unknown languages.
